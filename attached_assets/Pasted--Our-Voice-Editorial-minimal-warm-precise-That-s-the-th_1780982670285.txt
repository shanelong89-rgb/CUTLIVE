...Our Voice

Editorial-minimal, warm, precise. That's the three-word internal brief.

We're not a listings aggregator (TimeOut, Klook) and we're not a social app. We're a cultural curator — a human with taste who points you to things you'd actually love but might miss. Our voice should feel like a text from a friend who always knows what's good, in a city that makes discovery hard.

What that sounds like vs. what it doesn't:

"Hand-picked by Hong Kong's curators"
• Not our voice: "Over 172 events this week!"

"The best nights in HK are behind this wall"
• Not our voice: "Sign up to unlock unlimited access"

"We'll remind you before it sells out"
• Not our voice: "Create an account for exclusive benefits"

"Curated by Hong Kong locals"
• Not our voice: "Powered by AI"

"Don't miss your next great night out"
• Not our voice: "Don't miss out on amazing events!"

Warm but never desperate. Precise but never cold. We assume our audience is smart — we're not explaining what an art exhibition is, we're telling them why this one matters.

Current Copy Audit

The existing copy is actually surprisingly solid in places and breakable in others:

Working:
- "Don't miss your next great night out. Hand-picked by Hong Kong's curators." — warm, clear, human
- "Curated by Hong Kong locals — join free to save events and get reminded before they sell out" — clear value exchange
- "The best nights in HK are behind this wall" — confident, has edge

Not working:
- "Join Free" / "SEE EVERYTHING — IT'S FREE" — these say "free" twice but don't say what I actually get. Free is a price, not a benefit
- "Save events & get notified before they sell out" — the mid-page CTA is better but still functional, not magnetic
- The sign-in modal says "Welcome Back" to first-time visitors — small but wrong signal
- The blur wall copy is good but the CTA ("SEE EVERYTHING") is generic — "everything" is vague and not compelling

The One-Sentence Philosophy

Our voice says: "We did the work so you can just show up."

Every CTA should feel like an invitation to a better weekend, not a transaction. That's the difference between "Join Free" (transactional, cost-framed) and "Find your thing" (aspirational, benefit-framed)....
