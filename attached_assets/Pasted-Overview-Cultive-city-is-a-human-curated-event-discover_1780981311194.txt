Overview
Cultive.city is a human-curated event discovery platform for Hong Kong. The current design is strong — clean Swiss minimalism, good typography, solid visual hierarchy. The curated angle is the actual moat. The recommendations below focus on increasing account sign-ups and sharpening the messaging to drive FOMO and urgency.

1. Increasing Account Sign-Ups
1A. Add a Hero CTA Above the Fold
The only sign-up prompt today is the blur wall after scrolling past 10 events. A first-time visitor lands on 'What's On' with zero context of what to do. Add a persistent sticky CTA or a subtle hero banner — something like 'Get curated picks in your inbox' with a single email input. Captures people before they start browsing.

1B. Offer a No-Account Preview Tier
The hard blur wall is aggressive. A softer gate — 'Sign up to save events' or 'Set reminders so you never miss the ones you care about' — converts the mildly interested. Let people bookmark 3 events before hitting the wall; that investment makes them more likely to create an account to keep them.

1C. Hook Into the Event Detail Page
Every event detail page should have a floating CTA: 'Add to your Cultive calendar' or 'Notify me when this starts' — locked behind sign-up. Contextual conversion points at the moment of intent convert far better than generic 'join' banners.

1D. Email Capture Before Full Sign-Up
A single-field email input ('Notify me when good events come up in [neighbourhood]') is radically lower friction than email + password + Google auth. Collect the email, send a magic link, and you have them in the funnel.

1E. Use the Curation Angle as Social Proof
Every event is tagged with a curator name (e.g. ✦ SHANE). That is a feature most event apps do not have. Lead with it: 'Hand-picked by Hong Kong's curators. Join X members who never miss a good night out.'

2. Improving the Messaging
2A. Hero Line Rewrite
Current: 'Hong Kong's curated events. What's worth leaving the house for.' This is decent but passive. It describes what the site is, not what the user gains. Consider:
'Don't miss your next great night out.'
'The one thing you'll actually regret missing this week.'
'Your friends already bought tickets. You just haven't found out yet.'

2B. Blur Wall / Paywall Copy
Current: 'Join Cultive to see everything' — generic. This is your second-biggest messaging moment. Options:
'155 more events you'll kick yourself for missing. See them all.'
'You've scrolled past 10 tonight. Don't scroll past the one.'
'The best events are behind this wall. The FOMO is real.'

2C. Sign-Up Prompt Copy
Current: 'Free to join. See all events, save favourites, and submit your own.' Blander than unseasoned congee. Try:
'Curated for Hong Kong. Free to join. We'll remind you before it sells out.'

2D. Scarcity Signals
Add 'Only X spots left' badges on events that are likely to sell out (club nights, limited-capacity dinners, gallery openings). This drives sign-ups from FOMO alone — people will create an account just to see availability.

2E. Weekly Email Digest
'Your Weekly Cultive' — a digest of 3 hand-picked events sent weekly. Gate it behind account creation. The promise of curated picks landing in your inbox every Monday is a powerful sign-up driver.

3. Quick Tactical Wins
The 'Tickets' nav item is a ghost link for non-members. Change it to 'Get Early Access' and redirect to sign-up. People who click Tickets have purchase intent — don't waste that traffic.
The search bar is missing. If a user wants a specific artist or venue, they have to scroll through 165+ events. Add search in the header.
Event poster thumbnails are small. For a visual-heavy audience (art, design, culture), larger images would increase engagement. Consider a grid view toggle alongside the list view.
Truncation on mobile event titles is too aggressive. 'JUNE SESSIONS — HE...' forces users to click through. Surface the full title or use a secondary info line.
